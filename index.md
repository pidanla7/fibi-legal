# Privacy policy

*Last updated: May 2026*

Fibi is a fiber-tracking app built around a character named Herb. We try to collect as little data as possible, store it carefully, and give you straightforward control over what happens to it. This policy explains what we collect, why, and what your rights are.

If anything below is unclear, email **meetfibi@gmail.com**.

---

## What Fibi collects

**If you create an account (magic link or Apple Sign In):**

- Your email address (for authentication only — we don't send marketing email). If you sign in with Apple, you choose whether Apple shares your real email or a private-relay anonymized email — both work for Fibi.
- A stable user identifier scoped to Fibi — issued by Apple if you use Apple Sign In, or generated by Fibi's auth provider (Supabase) if you use magic link. We use this identifier to associate your data with your account.

**If you skip signup and use Fibi anonymously:**

- A randomly generated user identifier with no link to your real identity. You can convert an anonymous account to a full account at any time without losing your data.

**Onboarding answers (whichever path you choose):**

- Biological sex (with a "prefer not to say" option)
- Age bracket (under 30, 30–50, 50+)
- Activity level
- A calculated daily fiber goal derived from the above

**Logged fiber data:**

- The foods you log, their fiber grams, portion descriptions, the time you logged them, and the method (Quick log, recents, favourites, custom food, or Apple Health import)
- Streak counts, Herb's evolution stage, total journey days, and similar progress metrics

**Purchase status:**

- Whether you have completed the one-time in-app purchase that unlocks Herb's later evolution stages. This is stored as a true/false flag plus the timestamp of purchase.

**Apple Health data, if you grant permission:**

- Dietary fiber samples from Apple Health (read access). Fibi can also write your Fibi-logged fiber back to Apple Health (write access). Both permissions are requested separately and can be revoked any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Fibi.
- When Fibi reads a fiber sample from Apple Health, it stores that sample in your Fibi log on Fibi's servers (alongside your other logged foods) so the home screen total is accurate and so the cross-source dedup math can run server-side. Apple Health values are tied to your Fibi account but are never sold, shared with third parties, or used for advertising. Other Apple Health data types (calories, weight, sleep, heart rate, etc.) are NEVER read.

## What Fibi does NOT collect

Fibi does not collect:

- Photos, video, or images
- Voice recordings or audio
- Location, GPS coordinates, or IP-based location
- Your contacts, calendar, or other device data
- Browsing history or activity in other apps
- Health data beyond dietary fiber from Apple Health
- Any data from children under 13 (Fibi is not directed at children — see "Children" below)

## How Fibi uses what it collects

- **To run the app for you.** Your goal, logs, streak, and Herb's state need to persist across sessions and devices.
- **To dedupe data across apps.** When you log fiber in both Fibi and another HealthKit-aware app, Fibi uses the timestamps to avoid double-counting.
- **To show you progress.** Streaks, evolution stages, and the home screen total are computed from your logs.
- **To validate purchases.** Apple confirms your in-app purchase to Fibi's servers; we mark your account as unlocked.

Fibi does NOT:

- Sell or rent your data to third parties
- Use your data for advertising
- Profile you for ad targeting (we don't show ads)
- Train AI models on your data

## Where your data lives

Fibi's backend is hosted on **Supabase** (PostgreSQL with Row Level Security). Your data is isolated to your account — other users cannot read or modify it. Server-side rules enforce this regardless of what an attacker might try from a client.

Your iPhone also keeps a small amount of data locally for the app to function — your auth session token, onboarding flags, and similar app preferences live in iOS **SecureStore** (Apple's encrypted key-value store). This data is sandboxed to Fibi by iOS and is removed when you delete the app.

Purchase confirmation flows from **Apple's StoreKit** to **RevenueCat** (which handles cross-device unlock continuity) to Supabase.

Search queries you type into Quick log are sent to **USDA FoodData Central** (a US Department of Agriculture API) and, in a future release, **Open Food Facts** (an open-source branded foods database). These services receive only your search query — they do not receive your account ID, email, or any personally identifying information.

**Future analytics (planned, not yet active):** before public launch we will add **TelemetryDeck**, a privacy-first analytics service that receives anonymous usage events (e.g. "user opened Quick log", "user logged a food") so we can understand which features are used. TelemetryDeck does not receive your account ID, email, IP address, or any data that could identify you. This policy will not require an update when TelemetryDeck activates because it is pre-disclosed here.

## Data retention and deletion

- **Active accounts:** data persists for as long as your account exists.
- **Account deletion (signed-in accounts):** you can delete your account from Settings. Deletion cascades through all your data — logs, favourites, custom foods, streaks, evolution state — and the data is purged from our servers within 24 hours.
- **Data export:** request a JSON export of all your data by emailing **meetfibi@gmail.com**. The export is sent within 24 hours. (A self-serve "export my data" button in Settings is planned for v1.0 launch — once it ships, the request will be a single tap with the same 24-hour delivery SLA.)
- **Anonymous accounts (no email, no Apple Sign In):** anonymous sessions have no real-identity tie. If you delete the app, your on-device session token is removed and you lose access to that anonymous account permanently. The corresponding data row on our servers becomes orphaned — we have no way to associate it with you (or any future user) and it cannot be retrieved or re-attached. To explicitly request deletion of an orphaned anonymous account, you would need to retain enough information to identify it (your anonymous user ID is visible in Settings); without that, the orphaned data remains.

## Apple Health specifics

Fibi's relationship with Apple Health is bidirectional and entirely under your control:

- Fibi requests **read** permission for the "Dietary Fiber" data type only. We do not read calories, macros, weight, sleep, activity, or any other health type.
- Fibi requests **write** permission for "Dietary Fiber" so foods you log in Fibi appear in the Health app alongside your other nutrition data.
- You can grant either permission, both, or neither. You can change your decision at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Fibi.
- Apple Health values that Fibi reads are stored in your Fibi log on Fibi's servers (alongside your other logged foods) so the home screen total is accurate and the cross-source dedup math can run server-side. They are tied to your Fibi account but never sold, shared with third parties, or used for advertising.
- The samples Fibi writes to Apple Health stay on your iPhone unless you choose to back them up via iCloud Health, which is governed by Apple's iCloud privacy policy.

## Children

Fibi is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you are a parent and believe your child has used Fibi to create an account, email **meetfibi@gmail.com** and we will delete the account and associated data.

In the EU and UK, the equivalent age threshold is 16 under GDPR-K. The same protection applies: we do not knowingly collect data from anyone under that age.

## Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have legal rights over the data Fibi holds about you:

- **Access:** request a copy of your data by emailing **meetfibi@gmail.com** (or, once it ships, via Settings → "export my data").
- **Correction:** request correction of inaccurate data (most onboarding answers and logs are editable directly in the app).
- **Deletion:** delete your account from Settings, or email us to request deletion.
- **Portability:** data export delivers your data in JSON format, sent within 24 hours of request.
- **Withdrawal of consent:** revoke Apple Health permissions in iOS Settings; sign out or delete your account in Fibi.
- **Complaint:** if you believe Fibi has mishandled your data, you can complain to your local data protection authority. In the EU this is your national DPA; in the UK it is the ICO.

To exercise any of these rights, email **meetfibi@gmail.com** or use the in-app controls in Settings.

## International data transfer

Fibi's backend (Supabase) is hosted in a region we choose at project setup. If you use Fibi from a country other than where the database is hosted, your data is being transferred internationally. This transfer is governed by Supabase's standard contractual clauses for international data transfers under GDPR.
